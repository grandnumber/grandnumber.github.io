Courage finding & building courage

19th January 2014

This week we are looking at our values, how we live up to them & how to affirm them. Courage is hard for me to describe, to define what I mean. Courage, for me, is a deep willingness to acknowledge my fear, face that fear and still take action no matter how frightened I find myself. It is not being acknowledged as fearless or brave or tough - it is not about being acknowledged. It is about finding the ability to listen to my own wishes and still follow them in spite of fear. Not the most eloquent description - perhaps others can better describe courage for me:

“I'm not afraid of storms, for I'm learning to sail my ship.” —Louisa May Alcott Novelist

"He who is not courageous enough to take risks will accomplish nothing in life." —Muhammad Ali

"You will never do anything in this world without courage. It is the greatest quality of the mind next to honor." —Aristotle

"Bravery is the capacity to perform properly even when scared half to death." —Omar N. Bradley

So, if we accept that we have a common understanding of courage - how then to affirm it? The first step, for me, is reaffirming for myself that courage is not the absence of fear - it's ok to be frightened. After that it is allowing myself to feel that fear - not just accept it at an intellectual level. And that is the hardest part for me - truly feeling fear. I find that once I allow myself to be in the fear I can take action, I can embody courage. So finding the fear, naming it and most importantly, feeling it - is critical. Perhaps I can best affirm this, and convey my thinking in the words of another - John Wayne put it so well: "Courage is being scared to death -- and saddling up anyway"

So, here is to truly thinking and feeling before and during DBC - and saddling up anyway!
