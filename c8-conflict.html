Conflict or avoidance as it happens ...

24th January 2015

Ahhh - conflict! Having been brought up in an English/Scottish home conflict is something that was rarely expressed openly. (The WORST thing was being sent to "coventry" - not being spoken to at all for a period of time!). So it took me a while to learn about expressing and dealing with conflict in the open. Something of an ongoing life journey, like many things! So why is dealing with conflict so critical?

“10% of conflict is due to difference in opinion and 90% is due to the wrong tone of voice" —Anonymous, often attributed

Well, once a conflict occurs it is usually accompanied by anger and frustration... which can lead to raised voices and harsh words (or a withdrawl in a more passive expression). Which is why we need to recognise conflict and allow ourselves to acknowledge it before we lash out and compound the situation.

"All conflict we experience in the world, is a conflict within our own selves." —Brenda Shoshanna

And as I look inward, usually when there is conflict, there really is some form of conflict within. Did I have expectations that were unreasonable in this situation? Perhaps I was expecting too much? Or just expecting something from the wrong person. Or am I being stubborn about a point of view because I am afraid of trying things a different way? As the quote above points out - conflict occurs outwardly with other people, but also within ourselves.

"Peace is not the absence of conflict, but the ability to cope with it." —Dorothy Thomas

So, if we can learn to recognise, acknowlege and really stop to look inward can we avoid conflict? No. And in many ways we don't want to - but I believe that we can find healthier and less painful ways to try and cope with the conflict. Perhaps talking about the situation or perhaps removing ourselves from the situation for a time. And if it really is a conflict that cannot be resolved - then walking away and learning from it, so that it hopefully does not occur again in the future.
